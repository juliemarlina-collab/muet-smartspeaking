What is MUET SmartSpeaking?
🎓
Malaysian University English Test
Set by MPM under code 800/2. Required for university entry. Speaking is the speaking test component and focuses on individual presentation and group discussion.
⏱️
Total Duration: ~30 minutes
Task A: 2 min prep + 2 min speaking. Task B: 3 min prep + 8–12 min group discussion.
👥
Group Size: 2–4 Candidates
Two examiners observe throughout. You cannot see the examiners' marks during the test.
📊
Scored on 6 Criteria
Task Fulfilment, Fluency & Coherence, Vocabulary, Language Accuracy, Communicative Ability, Pronunciation.
💡
Start with Learn to study PREP and interaction skills. Then use Practice to record yourself. Check Results to track progress.
🎤 Task A — Individual Presentation
📄
One situation, four different points
All candidates read the same situation. Each is assigned a different point (A, B, C or D) to present.
⏱️
2 min prep → 2 min speaking
Write bullet notes only. No reading full sentences. Use PREP: Point → Reason → Example → Restate.
🏷️
Scored on Production
Task Fulfilment, Fluency, Vocabulary, Accuracy, Communicative Ability, Pronunciation.
👥 Task B — Group Discussion
🔗
Same topic as Task A always
A broader question + 5 mind-map ideas. You may support, oppose, or add your own ideas.
⏱️
3 min prep → 8–12 min discussion
Group MUST decide ONE best suggestion before time ends. Candidate A usually opens and closes.
🤝
Scored on Interaction
Same 6 criteria — but Interaction gets highest weight. Respond to others by name, ask questions.

Task A Guide
Individual Presentation
2 min prep · 2 min speaking · PREP structure
📋 Read this first!
🎯
PREP = Point → Reason → Example → Point (Restate). Follow this order every time. It is your speaking map!
P — Point · State your assigned position
Begin with your candidate point directly and clearly.
"I strongly believe that [your point] is the most important because…"
R — Reason · Explain WHY your point matters
Give ONE strong reason. One clear reason is better than three weak ones.
"This is because… / The main reason is that…"
E — Example · Prove it with a Malaysian example
Give ONE specific local example — a programme, statistic, or real event in Malaysia.
"For example, in Malaysia… / Studies in Malaysia show…"
P+ — Restate · Close confidently
Repeat your main position. End with "Thank you."
"Therefore, I strongly believe that [restate point]. Thank you."
How to Open Task A
1
Greet the examiner
"Good morning to the examiner and fellow candidates. I am Candidate [A/B/C/D]."
2
Refer to the situation briefly
"Based on the situation given regarding [topic]…"
3
State your point (P)
"I strongly believe that [your assigned point] is the most important because…"
4
Follow PREP, then conclude
Reason → Example → Restate. Always end with "Thank you."
Useful Task A Phrases
Opening
Good morning. I am Candidate A. I strongly believe that… In my opinion, the best way is…
Giving a Reason
This is because… The main reason is that… This is important because…
Giving an Example
For example, in Malaysia… A good example is… According to a study in Malaysia…
Conclusion
Therefore, I strongly believe that… In conclusion, [restate]. Thank you.
Model Answer · Candidate B · Estimated Band 4.5
Topic: Benefits of Technology in Education
Point: Accessing educational resources online
PPoint
Good morning to the examiner and fellow candidates. I am Candidate B. I strongly believe that using technology to access vast educational resources is the greatest benefit of technology in education today.
RReason
This is because students no longer need to rely solely on printed textbooks, which may be outdated. With a smartphone, they can instantly access up-to-date articles, e-books, and videos at any time.
EExample
For example, platforms like Google Scholar have allowed Malaysian university students, including those in rural areas, to complete research assignments at no extra cost.
P+Restate
Therefore, I strongly believe that providing students with open access to global knowledge through technology is the most significant benefit for Malaysian education. Thank you.
⭐
This model answer is about 165 words. Aim for 150–200 words in 2 minutes. Speak clearly — do not rush!
Task B Guide
Group Discussion
3 min prep · 8–12 min discuss · Must reach consensus!
🔗 Same topic as Task A always
🔗
Same topic rule: Task B ALWAYS continues from Task A. The group discusses a bigger question and must agree on ONE best answer before time ends.
4 Moves for a Great Task B
1
OPEN — Candidate A starts
Restate the question. Share your idea. Invite: "What do you think, Candidate B?"
2
BUILD — Each candidate contributes
Idea + reason + example. RESPOND to others — agree, add, or politely disagree. Use names!
3
CHALLENGE — Disagree once
One polite disagreement + one question raises your Interaction score significantly.
4
DECIDE — Reach consensus
"Shall we agree that [X] is the best?" The group MUST decide ONE best idea.
⚠️
If the group does NOT reach consensus, marks are lost. Make sure someone closes: "Based on our discussion, we agree that…"
✅ Agreeing
I completely agree with Candidate A because… That is a valid point. I would also add that… Building on what Candidate B said, I think… You are absolutely right. Furthermore…
🔄 Polite Disagreement
I see your point, however I believe… With respect, I have a different view… That may be true, but we should also consider… I understand your point, but I feel that…
❓ Asking / Inviting
Candidate C, what are your thoughts on this? Could you elaborate on that further? What do you think about this idea? Do you agree with Candidate A's point?
🤝 Reaching Consensus
Shall we agree that [X] is the best solution? Based on our discussion, we all agree that… In conclusion, our group decides that [X] is…
💬 Keeping Discussion Going
May I add something here? I would like to add that… Let us compare these two ideas. We are running out of time. Let us decide now.

I would like to begin. I think upgrading sports facilities is most effective. Candidate B, what is your view?OPENS + INVITES

Evidence-Based Teaching Strategies
🔴 Task Fulfilment — Most Critical
Students speak off-topic. Daily fix: drill PREP in exactly 4 sentences. Give 5 different Task Cards per class. Accept no answer that skips P-R-E-P structure.
🔴 Fluency & Coherence
Fillers ("um", "er") and long pauses. Fix: 60-second rapid speech bursts daily. Students record and listen back. Use Think-Pair-Share before every speaking task.
🟡 Vocabulary Range
Students repeat the same 50 words. Fix: MUET Word Wall per theme. Require 3 new words per session. Substitution drills: "good" → "beneficial / advantageous / constructive".
🟡 Language Accuracy
3 error types for low-proficiency: subject-verb agreement, tense, articles. Run 5-minute weekly grammar sprints. Focus on errors that impede communication first.
🟡 Interaction (Task B)
Students speak TO examiner, not EACH OTHER. Fix: Circle seating. Ban speaking without naming another candidate. Reward students who ask questions. 10-minute warm-up discussions every class.
🟢 Communicative Ability
Register improves quickly with role-play. Practise "audience shifts" — present to teacher, then peers, then imaginary examiner. Notice how language changes.
🟢 Pronunciation
Clarity over accent. Focus on word stress and sentence rhythm. Record and compare week-by-week. Use minimal pairs for 5 minutes before each practice.
